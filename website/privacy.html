Privacy

What Axon sees.
What it doesn't.

No surprises. No fine print. Here's exactly what's happening.

What Axon sees on your Mac

  • ✓Which apps you have open and for how long
  • ✓When you switch between apps
  • ✓How long your Mac has been active or idle
  • ✓Your calendar events — names and times only, not the content of the events
  • ✓A screenshot of your screen every 30 seconds when active. This is processed locally on your Mac by an AI vision model — the result (e.g. "user is writing code") is stored. The image itself is never stored or transmitted anywhere.

What Axon never sees

  • ✗The content of your documents, emails, or messages
  • ✗Your passwords or anything you type
  • ✗Your browser history or what websites you visit
  • ✗Your files or file contents
  • ✗Anything on other people's screens or devices

Where your data goes

Your behavioural patterns — app usage, focus time, drift scores — are stored in a secure database to power Axon's learning over time. This is what allows Axon to get more accurate the longer you use it.

Screenshots are processed locally on your Mac by an AI vision model. The result (e.g. "user is writing code in VS Code") is stored. The image itself is not stored, uploaded, or sent anywhere.

Your data is never sold. Never shared with third parties. Never used to train AI models. It exists solely to make Axon more accurate for you.

You're in control

You can delete all your data at any time by saying "Hey Axon, delete my data" or by removing the app. When you remove Axon, all local data is deleted automatically.

You can pause monitoring at any time from the menu bar icon. When paused, Axon stops all observation and goes silent.

Axon watches what apps you use and when.

It never reads what's inside them.

Your data stays yours.
